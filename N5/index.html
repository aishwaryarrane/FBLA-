Career SUMMARY

  • The purpose of the career summary is to show my career choice, description of the career, skills and education required, and future job outlook (e.g., monetary, advancement).

  • Description

    Data analytics is multidisciplinary field has strong roots in computer science, information science, mathematics, operations, and statistics. Topic areas include data organization and management, computer programming, data mining and machine learning, optimization, and statistical methods, used to both investigate past business performance and predictively model future performance. This career path can be applied in wide range of fields at companies that are committed to the use of data to gain insights about their business (including consulting, entrepreneurship, financial services, marketing, risk management, sales, social media, and technology).

  • Education & Skills

    The educational path for a data analyst is very interdisciplinary, therefore analysts can come from varied backgrounds. Employers generally look for a background in Computer Science, Economics, or Informatics. Since it is a growing field, universities are accommodating its rapid ascension by introducing Data Science majors. Universities such as the University of California, Berkeley just added a major in 2018 with domain emphasis in a variety of tracks such as : Economics, Quantitative Social Science, and Business and Industrial Analytics. It is important that Data Analysts have strong foundations in mathematics (Calculus I & II) as well as statistical methods. Other skills that are important to this career including communication and analytical skills.

  • Job Outlook

    The employment of analysts is projected to grow 27 percent from 2016 to 2026, much faster than the average for all occupations. As technology advances and companies seek efficiency and cost savings, demand for operations research analysis should continue to grow. In addition, the median pay annual wage for analysts was $81,390 in May 2017.
